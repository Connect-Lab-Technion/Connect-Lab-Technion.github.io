Zamir Martinez is now officially a PhD student after passing his candidacy exam. Zamir presented his research proposal, *Symmetry as a Structural Principle in Formation Control*, which explores how symmetry can be used to design sparse distributed formation-control laws for multi-agent systems. His ideas connect symmetry, rigidity, and matrix-weighted consensus to reduce sensing and communication requirements while enabling coordinated formation motions. Congratulations!
